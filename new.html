Walang Pasok: Class, gov't work suspensions for Nov. 10 due to 'Uwan'

Kristine Daguno-Bersamina - Philstar.com November 8, 2025 | 5:15pm

MANILA, Philippines (Updated 7:32 p.m.) — Several local government units have announced the suspension of classes and government work for Monday, November 10, amid the threat of Typhoon Uwan (international name: Fung-Wong). State weather bureau PAGASA warned that Uwan could reach super typhoon strength before hitting parts of Luzon. Heavy rain, strong winds and storm surge may affect even areas far from its path. Below is a running list of suspensions:


  • Caloocan City – all levels, public and private; government offices suspended except for essential personnel in disaster response
  • City of Manila – no face-to-face classes, all levels, public and private, alternative learning modes to be implemented
  • Las Piñas City – all levels, public and private
  • Malabon City – all levels, public and private; government offices suspended except for essential personnel in disaster response
  • Mandaluyong City – all levels, public and private; government offices suspended except for essential personnel in disaster response
  • Marikina City – all levels, public and private
  • Navotas City – all levels, public and private
  • Pasay City no face-to-face classes, all levels, public and private, alternative learning modes to be implemented
  • Pasig City – all levels, public and private
  • Quezon City – no face-to-face classes; alternative learning modes (synchronous/asynchronous) to be implemented
  • Taguig City – all levels, including school activities, suspended on November 9 to 10
  • Valenzuela City – all levels, public and private; government offices suspended except for essential personnel in disaster response

Katseye, Fil-Am Bruno Mars, 'Golden' highlight Pop Duo/Group Grammy contest

Kristofer Purnell - Philstar.com November 8, 2025 | 2:00pm



MANILA, Philippines — Lady Gaga and Sabrina Carpenter may be competing for three of the top Grammy Awards next year, but their fellow pop artists are making sure the competition is interesting all around. Joining Gaga and Sabrina in the Record and Song of the Year race is "Apt.," the hit track by Blackpink member Rose and Filipino-American singer-songwriter Bruno Mars. It makes Rose the first solo K-pop singer ever nominated for a Grammy, as past Korean nominees were groups BTS, some of its members for Album of the Year credits, and classical artists. The Fil-Am artist won his 16th Grammy last year when he and Gaga's collaboration "Die With A Smile" won Best Pop Duo/Group Performance. "Apt." is in that hotly contested category this year as it even features tracks from popular musical films — "Defying Gravity" from "Wicked" and "Golden" from "KPop Demon Hunters." Fans of the latter movie will be happy to see "Golden" also in the Song of the Year race and the likely leader in for Best Song Written for Visual Media, while David Guetta's remix of the song is up for Best Remixed Recording, Non-Classical. "Golden" is performed in the film by fictional girl group Huntr/x, featuring the singing voices of Ejae, Audrey Nuna, Rei Ami.
